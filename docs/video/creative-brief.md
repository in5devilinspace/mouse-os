# Mouse OS promo — creative brief & shot board

**The Quiet Command** — 24s. Designed by a media-production roundtable (creative director, motion designer, hook copywriter, growth marketer), synthesized by the director. Human/room shots generated with Fal AI (Kling v2 master, text-to-video); the abstract light beat + all cursor/caption/end-card overlays rendered locally to match the brand; composited with ffmpeg (see `tools/video/`).

**Logline.** One dark room at dawn, one person whose hands never move, and a whole computer that obeys their voice — stillness revealed not as limitation but as command, told through a single physical law: voice becomes light, and light becomes the pointer.

**Music.** Near-silent and reverent. A single sustained warm drone in deep register, one soft felt-piano note that never resolves into a melody, and room tone: breath, a faint dawn hum. One gentle amber confirmation tone as the only "event" sound. Absolutely no swell, no strings, no slow-clap arc — the restraint is the flex. The loudest thing in the film is the person's own quiet voice.

**Credits.** SPINE + title "The Quiet Command," the dawn-as-literal-amber-light throughline, and the closing dawn-flood reveal are the Creative Director's. The COLD-OPEN hand-signal amputation (still hands = power, not helplessness) and the "the room is quiet, the computer isn't" tension are the Hook Copywriter's. The film's governing physical law — voice becomes light, light becomes the cursor, with real deceleration weight and a machine-answers-back waveform handshake — is the Motion/3D Designer's, and the cursor-as-living-protagonist is theirs. The undeniable PROOF beat (wide shot, hands resting, real cursor glides to exactly where the voice said and clicks), the militant no-pity framing, and the plain amber-on-navy CTA ("free, offline, open-source, try tonight") are the Growth Marketer's. The split VO line "Computers assumed hands. / We didn't." is drawn from the product's emotional core and delivered across the Marketer's return beat and the Director's close.

## Overlay system
Everything the Fal model can't reliably render is a composited layer in the brand palette (navy #0A0E1A ground, amber #FFB454 signal, light text #E8ECF6, monospace UI). Three overlay systems run across the film: (1) THE CURSOR — a crisp amber arrow that is born from light in Shot 2's breath-filament, travels the abstract path in Shot 3, and lands/clicks on the real screen surface in Shot 4; it is always the same object, obeying real physics (decelerate, tiny settle-bounce, click ring). (2) TYPOGRAPHY — sparse monospace captions bottom-left with generous negative space: the spoken command appears as a live subtitle keyed to the diegetic voice, and single-word beats ('point.') punctuate. (3) UI ECHO — in Shot 4 only, real desktop windows/panels animate on the monitor surface in sync with the cursor landing, kept dim and warm so they read as consequence, not as a SaaS screen-recording. The end card is pure composite: amber arrow settling into navy negative space, tagline, then CTA lockup. Cursor motion is always eased and deliberate — never a bouncing mascot.
